In the introduction to Rez Life, David Treuer employed elaborate enumeration and factual evidence to argue that Native Americans are not separate individuals from American society but significant contributors to American culture, economy, and military.

Treuer first introduces the geographical presence of Native Americans. By enumerating locations from "the middle of the desert" to "Niagara Falls", he illustrates the wide spread of Indian reservations. He also mentions the scale of reservations - huge ones are "bigger than the state of Rhode Island", and altogether the reservations make up "2.3 percent of the land in the United States". Comparing the area of reservations to that of a legal state, Treuer allows readers to have a clear impression of the actual size of Native lands. These facts put great emphasis on the physical significance of the Indian reservations, preventing the readers from considering them as negligible areas.
